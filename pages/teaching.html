My Personal Kanban 2.0: Local Project Management

Category: Software Development / Productivity

Most modern project management and Kanban board services run on the cloud, often imposing restrictive limitations on file upload sizes, storage quotas, and feature access. To address these limitations, I designed and built My Personal Kanban 2.0—a native, lightweight desktop application that runs entirely locally on your computer.

The application leverages a classic three-column Kanban system (To Do, In Progress, and Done) with smooth drag-and-drop state transitions. Key highlights of the project include:

  • Local File-Oriented Storage: Attaching files (such as PDF reports, code scripts, or images) to a task automatically generates a dedicated folder structure within your system's Documents directory, keeping your project assets clean and organized.
  • Task & Subtask Hierarchy: Users can break down larger goals into granular subtasks, complete with independent checkboxes, deletion safety mechanisms, and custom file attachments.
  • Hourly Planner & Quick Notes: Features a built-in interactive daily timeline (running from 6:00 AM to midnight) alongside a rich-text formatting panel for checklists, reminders, and persistent markdown-like notes.

The Book Corner: Essential Readings

Category: Literature / Reference Library

A curated selection of books, textbooks, and reference manuals that have significantly shaped my scientific understanding and technical skills over the years.

Principles of Plasma Discharges and Materials Processing by M. A. Lieberman & A. J. Lichtenberg

The absolute bible of low-temperature plasma physics. Its rigorous mathematical derivations and deep physical insights are indispensable for anyone working on gas discharges and materials processing.

Plasma Chemistry by Alexander Fridman

A comprehensive guide that bridges thermodynamics, chemical kinetics, and gas discharges, offering invaluable assistance for modern plasma chemistry modeling.

Introduction to Electrodynamics by David J. Griffiths

Perhaps the most beautifully written textbook in physics. Griffiths manages to make the complex world of Maxwell's equations intuitive, engaging, and clear.

Custom Ergonomic Mouse Design & Development for CTS Prevention

Topic: Ergonomic Product Design, CAD Modeling & 3D Printing

A step-by-step presentation detailing the creation of a custom vertical ergonomic mouse. The project explores wrist anatomy and Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS) causes, covers 3D CAD design using Autodesk Inventor, and culminates in 3D printing and assembling a functional prototype designed to maintain a natural, strain-free wrist posture.

Solving the 2D Laplace Equation with Finite Difference Method in MATLAB

Topic: Computational Mathematics, Finite Difference Method & MATLAB App Development

An educational walkthrough on solving the 2D Laplace Equation with Dirichlet boundary conditions. The presentation covers the mathematical discretization process, the derivation of derivatives using the Finite Difference Method to construct the stiffness matrix, and the development of an interactive MATLAB App Designer interface to solve and visualize the resulting potential distribution.

How a Photomultiplier Tube (PMT) Works

Topic: Physics, Optoelectronics, Photoelectric Effect & Signal Amplification

An educational animation illustrating the fundamental principles and inner workings of a Photomultiplier Tube (PMT). The video explains how incident light strikes a photosensitive photocathode to release electrons via the photoelectric effect, which are then accelerated and multiplied through a series of dynodes to produce a highly measurable electrical signal.
